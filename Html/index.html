Frequently Asked Questions

Why employment rate is so low in India?

Employment rate is so low in India, According to one of the prominent leaders, the causes are  Lack of vocational skill or low educational level of the working population.  Legal complexitites,inadequate state law, low infrastructural financial and mark linkages to small business.  The main cause of structural unemployment is the education provided in schools and colleges are not as per the current requirement of the industries.  The huge work force of the country is associated with the informal sector because of the lack of education or skill, and this data is not captured in employment statistics.

How can we increase employment?

The main ways by which we can increase employment in our country are:  By providing cheap credit facilities, and crop insurance to farmers can create employment.  By spending more money on transport and storage so that more people can be employed.  By creating more dams, water canals, a lot of employment can be generated in agriculture sector.  Most importantly, technical training, vocational guide to unemployed youth for self employment.

What is skills development and how it will help in the rate of the employment?

Skills development is the process of identifying your skill gaps and then developing and honing these skills.It is important because your skills determine your ability to execute your plan with success. It affects the rate of employment in positive ways  It raises productivity of workers.  Leads to higher wages  Empower people to develop their capacities and seize employment.
